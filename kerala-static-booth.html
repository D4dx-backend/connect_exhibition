About Kerala

Kerala, located on the lush Malabar Coast of India, is famous for its serene backwaters, swaying coconut palms, mist-covered hill stations, spice-scented plantations, and vibrant cultural traditions.

From the tranquil houseboats drifting along the Alleppey backwaters to the emerald tea gardens of Munnar and the wildlife of Wayanad and Periyar, every corner of Kerala offers a different shade of beauty.

Natural & Cultural Highlights

Kerala combines landscapes, flavors, and traditions. Explore a few of its iconic experiences:

Backwaters of Alleppey

Sail on traditional houseboats through peaceful canals lined with coconut trees and village life. Sunrise and sunset here are unforgettable.

Munnar Hills

Rolling hills covered in tea gardens, cool weather, and clouds that seem to kiss the earth. A perfect escape for nature lovers.

Beaches of Kovalam & Varkala

Golden sands, cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea, and glowing sunsets paint a picture of calm and romance.

Cultural Rhythms

Witness Kathakali dance, temple festivals, Theyyam rituals, and taste authentic Kerala Sadhya served on banana leaves.
